Butter Paneer Masala

Butter Paneer Masala holds significant cultural and culinary importance in India. As a popular vegetarian dish, it caters to the diverse dietary preferences in the country, making it a staple on restaurant menus and home kitchens alike. The rich and creamy texture, combined with the succulent paneer (cottage cheese) cubes, creates a satisfying and indulgent experience.

Beyond its delicious taste, Butter Paneer Masala symbolizes the fusion of traditional Indian flavors with a touch of modernity. It has become a representation of the country's culinary adaptability and the evolution of its gastronomic landscape. Often served during celebrations and special occasions, this dish has earned its place as a beloved classic, embodying the essence of comfort and festivity in Indian cuisine.

Ingredients

  1. Paneer (Indian cottage cheese): 250 grams, cut into cubes
  2. Onions: 2, finely chopped
  3. Tomatoes: 3, pureed
  4. Ginger-Garlic Paste: 1 tablespoon
  5. Green Chilies: 2, finely chopped
  6. Cashew Nuts: 10-12, soaked in warm water
  7. Butter: 3 tablespoons
  8. Oil: 2 tablespoons
  9. Cumin Seeds: 1 teaspoon
  10. Turmeric Powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  11. Red Chili Powder: 1 teaspoon
  12. Coriander Powder: 1 teaspoon
  13. Garam Masala: 1/2 teaspoon
  14. Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves): 1 teaspoon, crushed
  15. Fresh Cream: 3 tablespoons
  16. Coriander Leaves: For garnish
  17. Salt: To taste

Steps

  • Heat 2 tablespoons of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, sauté for another minute.
  • Add the pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the masala
  • Meanwhile, blend soaked cashew nuts with a little water to make a smooth paste. Add this paste to the tomato-onion mixture and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  • Add the paneer cubes and cook for 2-3 minutes, allowing them to absorb the flavors.
  • Sprinkle garam masala and crushed kasuri methi. Stir in the fresh cream and remaining butter. Mix gently.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve the Butter Paneer Masala hot with naan or rice. Enjoy!
