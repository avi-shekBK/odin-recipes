Chana Masala

Chana Masala, a cherished dish in Indian cuisine, is a tantalizing blend of chickpeas infused with aromatic spices. To prepare this flavorful vegetarian delight, onions, tomatoes, and green chilies are sautéed to perfection in oil, forming a fragrant base. A harmonious medley of spices, including cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala, is then introduced, creating a symphony of flavors. The heart of the dish lies in the tender chickpeas, either canned or pre-soaked, which simmer in the richly spiced gravy until they absorb the essence of the spices. Garnished with fresh coriander leaves, Chana Masala is a protein-packed and wholesome dish that pairs splendidly with rice or various Indian breads, offering a culinary journey through the vibrant and diverse tapestry of Indian flavors.

Ingredients

  • Chickpeas (Chana): 1 cup (canned or pre-soaked)
  • Onions: 2, finely chopped
  • Tomatoes: 3, finely chopped or pureed
  • Green Chilies: 2, finely chopped
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: 1 tablespoon
  • Cumin Seeds: 1 teaspoon
  • Coriander Powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Turmeric Powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Red Chili Powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Turmeric Powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Garam Masala: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Amchur (Dry Mango Powder): 1/2 teaspoon
  • Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves): 1 teaspoon, crushed
  • Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves): 1 teaspoon, crushed
  • Oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Salt: To taste

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, sautéing for an additional minute.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes or tomato puree. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil begins to separate from the masala.
  5. Introduce the dry spices: coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and amchur. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add the cooked or canned chickpeas and mix thoroughly, allowing them to absorb the flavors.
  7. Pour in a cup of water, adjust salt, and let the Chana Masala simmer for 10-15 minutes until it reaches a desirable consistency.
  8. Crush kasuri methi between your palms and sprinkle it over the dish for added aroma.Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve the Chana Masala hot, paired with rice or Indian bread of your choice. Enjoy!
